Most versions of Lex Luthor have been successful at their ambitions - for a time. But eventually, one of the constants of the character - his never-ending ambition and drive to prove himself the best and the brightest in the world - gets him beaten, shamed, and defeated. But one version of the character was able to step out of the spotlight and become the man behind the world's most powerful people - and won.

The short story "Rockumentary," by Bronwyn Carlton, Greg Luzniak, Anibal Rodriguez, Ken Lopez, and Noelle Giddings, introduces a world where Lex Luthor became a massive music mogul instead of a super-villain, quickly turning his version of the DC Universe into one dominated by rock versions of the Justice League -- and in the process, becoming one of the most successful versions of Lex in the entire Multiverse.

Don’t Download Anything From An Untrusted Source It’s important to be careful about what you download onto your computer since most malware and spyware is willingly put onto the target’s computer without them fully understanding what it is they’ve just done. Always check the file extensions of what’s being downloaded to make sure everything adds up.

For instance, if you’re downloading songs, they should be in an audio format such as .mp4 and not as an executable such as a .exe. These executable files can install just about anything on your computer while being extremely difficult to remove once installed.

Spotify raises limit of offline songs to 10.000 per device

All the reasons to avoid purchasing an Iphone

Published Jun 18, 2021
Published Jun 18, 2021

Spotify is improving of one of its most annoying, arbitrary limits. The music streaming app has increased the cap for maximum number of offline downloads, which was previously stuck at 3,333 songs per device (on a maximum of three devices).

As reported by Rolling Stone, that limit has now been raised to 10,000 songs — and you can download that many tunes on five different devices instead of just three. So you’re looking at 50,000 in all. “At Spotify, we’re always working on improving the experience for our users,” a company spokesperson told Rolling Stone.
